Meet the new librarian

Hello from St. Croix, U.S. Virgin Islands


Welcome one and all to my corner of the internet. My name is Jordan Albany Toussaint and I am on a track to become an elemntary school librarian and media specialist. Currently, I am a fourth grade teacher here on the island of St. croix where I grew up. If you are unfamiliar with the Caribbean region, the U.S. Virgin Islands is made up of four main islands of which St. Croix is the largest and affectionately called the "Big island" or "Twin City" for its two grid-like twin like cities. The U.S. Virgin Islands not to be confused with the British Virgin Islands was purchasd on March 31, 1917 by the United States from Denmark for a whopping $25 million in gold. Seven Flags have flown over St. Croix, each adding their piece to our history.

Fun fact! We have reatined many Danish customs like driving on the left side of the road.

Growing up, I was convinced that I wanted to be medical doctor and followed that path into college. I quickly realized it was not for me, and majored in elementeray education K-6 at Barry University in Miami Shores, FL and graduated with a TESOL and reading endorsements. While I had the opportunity to teach in Miami after my student teaching, I chose to return home to teach. After a few years the local oil refinery shut down and enrollment at my private school dwindled. I used this opportunity to relocated to Charlotte, NC and taught third grade there before returning back to St. Croix, where I have been teaching for the past ten years at the Pearl B. Larsen K-8 school. For more of my professional experiences view my resume above.
